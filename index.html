At The Cinema

(Quick Reviews)

Audition is a strange movie. It starts off semi harmless a guy's son tells him he looks old and he should remarry. Following his son's advice the man and his friend hold a fake movie audition that's really a front for him to interview a potential new wife. Sounds like the plot of an 80's rom com but this is more of an experimental or gore flick. The hard cuts and vast tonal shifts throughout the movie might aggravate some but it added to the unease and tension. Despite the third act being hard to follow, this is where it gets experimental, Audition was overall entertaining and if you're the kind of person that laughs at scary movies you'll get some giggles in as well.

When I saw the trailer for The Foreigner I thought it was going to be 2 hours of Jackie Chan beating the crap out of people. In reality the film isn't really about Jackie going on a rampage while looking for answers. Its really about Pierce Brosnan a 'former' IRA member turned politician trying to make political moves while this rightfully so, madman Jackie looms over him. The plot wasn't too convoluted like political dramas tend to be. It was pretty cool to see Jackie fighting a bit dirty, smashing people through tables and cracking bottles over heads. The film was very chatty though and after the conflict was resolved it dragged for a bit. I'd suggest catching this one on tv if you like. But if you were looking for pure action you're better of watching a montage of the fights on YouTube.
